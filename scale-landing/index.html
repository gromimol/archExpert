When to Engage
When technology consulting is needed?

Technology consulting is required when technologies, processes, and the team no longer match the speed and scale of the business.

Below are typical situations in which companies seek support to regain control and prepare the system for growth.

Growth increases the load but does not produce results

The company grows, the team expands, but development speed declines. This is a sign of architectural and process constraints that do not scale and begin to hold back business development.

The first implementation phase has failed

The system is inconvenient to use, users are dissatisfied, the money has already been spent, and the result is far from expectations. The work was done quickly under deadline pressure, and the outcome is a solution that no one uses. The task is to bring the product into a working state.

Technical debt becomes a source of business risk

The system grows, multiple approaches and tools appear, and agreements rely on personal communication. Any change becomes risky, and responsibility becomes blurred.

Architecture loses coherence and manageability

The system grows, multiple approaches and tools appear, and agreements rely on personal communication. Any change becomes risky, and responsibility becomes blurred.

Plans for new products or major changes appear

Before expanding functionality, it is necessary to ensure that the system can withstand additional load and change, otherwise the new product may become a point of failure.

Costs grow faster than profit

The IT budget is nontransparent: many solutions but little value. Money goes into maintaining disorder instead of development.

Data is no longer reliable

Different teams rely on different metrics, numbers diverge, and there is no single source of truth. This is the result of fragmented architecture and the absence of clear data ownership rules.

The product loses usability and manageability

More and more functionality is added, but the interaction logic becomes more complex. The user experience deteriorates, and product maintenance becomes expensive and slow.

FAQ
Frequently asked questions
How long does the work take

The timeline depends on the company’s scale and the condition of its systems. On average, it takes from 6 to 10 weeks: diagnostics, design, solution testing, and preparation for implementation.

Is this a replacement for an audit?

It is broader than an audit. We not only analyze the current state but also design the target architecture, develop a change plan, and support implementation.

Can we continue development in parallel?

Yes. The work is structured so that it does not block ongoing releases. All changes are aligned with the owners of the corresponding areas.

Do you work only with architecture, or do you address processes and teams as well?

Fully. We assess architecture, infrastructure, processes, ownership, and organizational factors that affect product speed and resilience.

Can you help implement the proposed changes?

Yes. Upon request, we support implementation: from consulting and quality control of technical decisions to full change support.

How do we know if we need this work now?

If development speed is declining, releases are unstable, architecture is expanding uncontrollably, costs grow faster than profit, and user complaints or failure risks appear - this is a signal to begin.

What does the final result look like?

You receive a structured report on the system’s state, prioritized risks, the target architecture, recommendations, an implementation plan, and support throughout execution.

Do you work under an NDA?

Yes. Confidentiality is a mandatory condition.

How much does your work cost?

The cost depends on the company’s scale, the number of systems, the maturity of the architecture, and the required depth of involvement. The estimate is provided after the initial meeting.
